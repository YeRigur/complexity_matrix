Three dimensions of complexity

The model separates three different sources of effort variation:

Difficulty = how hard?
Volume = how much?
Unknown = how uncertain?

This makes the model more transparent than using one vague concept of "complexity."

Scoring formula

Each dimension is scored 1 (Low), 2 (Medium), or 3 (High).

Complexity Score = Difficulty + Volume + Unknown

Score range: 3 (simplest) → 9 (most complex).

Mapping to complexity bands

3–4 = Low 5–6 = Medium 7–8 = High 9 = Highest (H+)
H+ (Highest) should be treated as exception-grade: closely governed, sometimes has different cost, time-based handling via estimation and planning.

Key principles

Difficulty ≠ Volume. A large volume of simple work can still be lower difficulty than a small amount of very complex work.
Unknown ≠ Difficulty. A task can be technically simple but full of unknowns (unclear requirements, unstable environment).
If complexity cannot be assessed with confidence because the request is too unstable or too unclear, it should not be forced into the model. Mark it as exception → time-based estimation → separate governance.
